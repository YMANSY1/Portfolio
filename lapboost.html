Login and Signup Screen

A screen allowing users to make an account or login to their existing accounts. Basic authentication practices like detecting similar emails and phone numbers on signup and ensuring the fields include appropriate emails and phone numbers were used to ensure a safe and secure experience.

Service Requests Progression

Before: The initial service request form required users to select each service individually, navigating to separate screens to complete forms for every service. This fragmented approach resulted in:
  • Poor user experience due to repetitive navigation
  • Excessive database entries from single-service orders
  • No ability to combine services/items in one transaction
After: The redesigned system introduces a unified cart experience allowing:
  • Multi-service selection in a single session
  • Combined service requests and marketplace purchases
  • Streamlined data structure reducing database bloat
This redesign streamlined workflows through modern UX patterns while establishing a foundation for scalable service management ‚Äì directly addressing core pain points from the original implementation.

Marketplace Progression

Before: The original marketplace restricted users to single-item purchases with fixed quantities, resulting in:
  • Frustrating checkout loops for multiple items
  • Inventory management challenges from fragmented orders
  • No quantity customization or bulk purchasing
After: Enhanced marketplace features now enable:
  • Multi-item cart system with adjustable quantities
  • Combined purchases across product categories
  • Ability to increase quantity outside of the cart screen by repeatedly pressing the add to cart button the requisite amount of times
  • Smart inventory deduction with real-time updates
This overhaul introduced essential e-commerce functionality while maintaining the platform's repair-service focus - creating a cohesive shopping experience that mirrors modern retail standards.

Checkout Process Upgrade and Cart System

Cart System: Now there is an integrated cart system allowing the user to select both items and services. the user has to specify a specific device of theirs for the service to be done on and specify the quantity of the marketplace item.
Checkout Screen: Now the user gets taken to a final checkout screen where their cart is displayed to them, allowing them to change their address, stipulate whether or not they need delivery, and see their final calculated total. After confirmation the order is recorded in the database.

Settings Screen

The settings screen allows the user to add their devices for services and alter their data including their email, password, address, phone number, and more. The user may also delete their account or log out is they wish. This provides an easy interface for users to view and edit their credentials.

Final Thoughts

Working on the LapBoost app was an intense but rewarding experience that pushed me to refine both my technical and problem-solving skills. I learned a lot about separation of privileges, ensuring that managers, technicians, and customers each had the right level of access to the system. Database design was another key challenge‚Äîstructuring an MySQL database to handle both service orders and marketplace purchases while keeping everything efficient and scalable. Integrating the database with Flutter was a major part of the process, especially handling real-time updates for order tracking. One of the biggest improvements was adding a cart system, allowing customers to place multiple orders at once, whether for services or marketplace items. The UI also evolved significantly, making the process smoother and more intuitive. Completing all of these major revisions in just three days under a tight deadline really proved to me that I can work effectively under pressure while still delivering a solid product. And to top it all off, LapBoost won 1st place in the ECEN424 course ranking at the 19th Undergraduate Research Forum (UGRF), which was an incredible way to wrap up the project!
